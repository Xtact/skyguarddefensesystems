O.T.I.S. Vision Architecture

Geometry Over Photons. Structure Over Surface.

A proprietary four-stage computer vision pipeline developed by SkyGuard Defense Systems and InSitu Labs. O.T.I.S. is agnostic to colorspace, illumination, and adversarial surface camouflage. If a target occupies physical volume, we isolate the geometry and we lock on.

The Threat: FlyTrap & Distance-Pulling Attacks

Modern Autonomous Target Tracking (ATT) systems are fundamentally vulnerable to adversarially engineered optical patterns. If your interceptor relies on surface-level photon reflection — whether it is parsing RGB, tracking HSV/HSL contours, relying on YCbCr video transmission, or utilizing shallow Near-Infrared (NIR) — it is operating on a spoofable medium.

FlyTrap — NDSS 2026 / UC Irvine

The FlyTrap adversarial umbrella is not a simple visual illusion. It is a spatiotemporal Distance-Pulling Attack (DPA) that maintains consistent motion signatures across frames, specifically engineered to defeat behavioral anomaly defenses. Defeated defenses include VOGUES, PercepGuard, and VisionGuard. FlyTrap is the current published baseline. Tomorrow, adversaries will deploy dynamic E-ink camouflage, localized holographic noise, and adaptive multispectral cloaking — all spatiotemporally consistent, all designed to survive every behavioral filter a standard 2D camera stack can apply.

SkyGuard recognized that relying purely on camera-derived colorspace data was a structural flaw in the intercept pipeline — not a configuration problem. The fix required operating on a different substrate entirely: real spatial geometry derived from stereo depth measurement, not inferred distance from monocular optical cues.

O.T.I.S. // Optical Threat Isolation System

O.T.I.S. is a ROS-native C-UAS computer vision suite that strips adversarial optical noise and builds a continuously updated structural mesh of the environment. When a spatial anomaly is isolated, O.T.I.S. overrides standard camera feeds, locks a geometric skeleton onto the true center of mass, and interfaces directly with the drone's payload mechanisms to deliver a targeted kinetic intercept — before the hostile operator can bring hardware into capture range.

The pipeline is deterministic and four-stage. Each stage feeds the next; no single stage is sufficient alone, and no adversarial surface treatment defeats all four.

STAGE 01

Spectral Delamination Matrix (SDM)

Channel-decoupled filtering across RGB, HSV, YUV, and NIR independently. SDM compares channel-level statistical signatures against known attack fingerprints and outputs a spectrally sanitized frame where adversarial camouflage patterns are mathematically inert to downstream stages.

STAGE 02

Kinetic Contour Mapping (KCM)

Edge extraction via gradient-magnitude and phase-congruency analysis. Phase congruency is illumination-invariant — it responds to structural boundaries defined by geometry and material discontinuity, not brightness. Printed adversarial patterns generate brightness edges; they do not generate phase-congruent structural edges.

STAGE 03

Volumetric Stereo-Triangulation (VST)

Live depth-perception engine generating a continuously updated spatial mesh from the stereo camera pair. Per-pixel real depth data replaces monocular distance inference entirely. Distance-manipulation tactics are inert once the system operates on true geometry rather than inferred cues.

STAGE 04

Structural Skeletonization

3D point-cloud triangulation and wireframe fitting extracts the target's underlying geometric skeleton from the VST depth mesh. The spoofed exterior disappears from the tracking matrix. What remains is the true volumetric structure — locked, center-of-mass confirmed, intercept vector calculated.

ROS 2 Native. O.T.I.S. is hardware-agnostic and integrates directly with standard ROS 2 navigation and payload interfaces. The pipeline runs fully onboard — no cloud dependency, no human-in-the-loop latency between acquisition and intercept.

Why Geometry Defeats Every Optical Attack

Every optical spoofing attack in the published literature — FlyTrap, retroreflective cloaking, adversarial texture projection, multispectral deception — operates on the same assumption: that the tracking system's ground truth is a camera frame. That assumption is correct for every RGB, NIR, thermal, and behavioral-anomaly system currently fielded.

O.T.I.S. breaks that assumption at the architectural level. By the time the skeletonization stage executes, the system has never made a decision based on what the target looks like. It has only made decisions based on where the target is, how large its structural volume is, and where its center of mass sits in three-dimensional space. A printed umbrella, an E-ink panel, a holographic projector — none of these tools add or remove physical volume. They cannot defeat a system that does not look at surfaces.
