Chronicles of Sensor Spoofing

Era 2: GPS/GNSS Spoofing

As drones moved into wide-area surveillance and BVLOS operations, they became critically dependent on satellite navigation. This dependency created a massive hardware vulnerability. Adversaries stopped trying to confuse the camera and started attacking the platform's fundamental understanding of where it was located.

The Exploit: Overpowering the Faint Signal

Drones rely on faint GPS/GNSS satellite signals to calculate their coordinates. An attacker broadcasts a fake GPS signal on the same frequency using a powerful ground transmitter. This is a brute-force hardware takeover. The drone's receiver, programmed to lock onto the strongest signal, accepts the fake coordinates and rejects the true satellite data.

Hijacking the PNT Matrix

This is a fundamental failure of the Position, Navigation, and Timing (PNT) matrix. By controlling the GPS input, the attacker can force the drone to believe it is miles away from its true location, steering it into hostile territory or completely neutralizing its tracking matrix.

SkyGuard Mitigation: Visual Odometry & VST

Our primary software defense is Visual Odometry. We teach the drone to look at the ground and navigate by matching physical landmarks in real-time, bypassing the invisible radio spectrum. O.T.I.S. cross-references spoofed GPS data against the VST spatial mesh. If the GPS claims the drone is over open water, but the spatial mesh shows a dense urban center, O.T.I.S. rejects the GNSS input and switches to full Visual Inertial Odometry (VIO).
