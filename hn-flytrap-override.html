Show HN: Bypassing optical spoofing (DPA/Flytrap) with live stereo depth-mesh intercept

By SkyGuard_Lead | SkyGuard Defense Systems (InSitu Labs) | Denver, CO

Modern Autonomous Target Tracking (ATT) systems are fundamentally vulnerable. If your interceptor relies on surface-level photon reflection — whether it is parsing RGB, tracking HSV/HSL contours, relying on YCbCr video transmission, or even utilizing shallow Near-Infrared (NIR) — it is operating on a spoofable medium. It can be blinded, tricked, or hijacked by adversarially engineered patterns that are specifically designed to be consistent enough to defeat anomaly detection.

The current published iteration of this vulnerability is the FlyTrap adversarial umbrella, a Distance-Pulling Attack (DPA) presented at NDSS 2026 by researchers at UC Irvine. FlyTrap is not a simple visual illusion — it is a spatiotemporal adversarial attack that maintains consistent motion signatures across frames, engineered to defeat behavioral anomaly defenses including VOGUES, PercepGuard, and VisionGuard. Against off-the-shelf tracking systems, it demonstrated a 100% attack success rate on DJI Mini 4 Pro, DJI NEO, and HoverAir X1.

And FlyTrap is just the baseline. Tomorrow, adversaries will deploy dynamic E-ink camouflage, localized holographic noise, and adaptive multispectral cloaking — all spatiotemporally consistent, all designed to survive every behavioral filter a 2D camera stack can apply.

At SkyGuard, we bypass the visual feed entirely. We are agnostic to the illusion. We don't care what spectrum an adversary broadcasts in, or what colorspace a legacy camera uses to interpret it. If it occupies physical volume, we isolate the geometry and we lock on.

The O.T.I.S. Override Pipeline

To achieve the INTERCEPT LOCK you see in the override window, we push the sensor data through a deterministic, four-stage pipeline:

1. Spectral Delamination Matrix (SDM)

A channel-decoupled filtering module that separates and independently analyzes adversarial spectral content across all standard and extended color spaces (RGB, HSV, YUV, NIR). Rather than processing a fused composite frame, SDM operates on each channel independently, identifying and suppressing high-contrast spoofing patterns and adversarial noise by comparing channel-level statistical signatures against known attack fingerprints. The output is a spectrally sanitized representation where camouflage artifacts are mathematically inert to downstream stages.

2. Kinetic Contour Mapping (KCM)

Once spectral noise is stripped, KCM initiates an edge-extraction pass using gradient-magnitude and phase-congruency analysis. Phase congruency is illumination-invariant — it responds to genuine structural boundaries defined by geometry and material discontinuity, not brightness discontinuity. This makes it specifically resistant to 2D projections and printed adversarial patterns, which generate brightness edges but not phase-congruent structural edges. True physical boundaries are isolated and passed to the depth stage.

3. Volumetric Stereo-Triangulation (VST)

This is where we break the DPA and future-proof the intercept. VST is our live depth-perception engine that generates a continuously updated spatial mesh from the stereo camera pair. Distance-manipulation tactics become useless once the system is operating on real, per-pixel depth data rather than inferred target distance from monocular cues.

Our disparity energy minimization enforces structural smoothness via a regularization term while aggressively preserving the hard boundaries identified by KCM. The functional is a weighted variant of the standard semi-global energy formulation:

$$E(\mathbf{p}) = \sum_{\mathbf{q} \in \mathcal{N}(\mathbf{p})} w_{\mathbf{q}} \left\| I_L(\mathbf{q}) - I_R(\mathbf{q} - \mathbf{d}(\mathbf{p})) \right\|^2 + \lambda \|\nabla \mathbf{d}(\mathbf{p})\|^2$$
where d(p) is the disparity field, wq are KCM-derived edge-aware weights that upweight neighbors across structural boundaries, and λ is the smoothness regularization coefficient — tuned per-scene to balance edge sharpness against disparity noise in low-texture adversarial surfaces.

4. Structural Skeletonization (The "X-Ray" Override)

The final stage. Using the depth mesh from VST, O.T.I.S. performs 3D point-cloud triangulation and wireframe fitting to extract the target's underlying geometric skeleton. The spoofed exterior disappears entirely from the tracking matrix — what remains is the true volumetric structure of the target. The system locks a red wireframe precisely onto the true center of mass to calculate a zero-latency kinetic intercept vector. No optical surface needed. No colorspace matters. Pure geometry.
