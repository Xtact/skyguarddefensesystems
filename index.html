O.T.I.S.

Optical Threat Isolation System

Most detection systems try to learn what a threat looks like. OTIS works differently — it knows what a threat must behave like, based on the laws of physics. That distinction changes everything.

At the heart of OTIS is a threat model catalog: a structured set of physical parameter envelopes developed from the ground up by SkyGuard Labs using established laws of physics, engineering data, and real-world field observation. These envelopes define the precise boundaries of how any threat-class object must look, move, and radiate heat given its size, speed, and mechanical design. No guesswork. No statistical pattern matching on images. The rules come from physics — and physics doesn't change.

This catalog comes baked into OTIS from day one. But OTIS doesn't stop there. Every time OTIS observes and confirms an object — across every deployment, every integration, every unit in the field — it refines its statistical confidence within those physics-grounded boundaries. It isn't learning new rules. It is getting more precise about the rules it already knows, informed by real-world observations drawn from every OTIS system operating anywhere. The result is a platform that grows more accurate and more confident with every interaction — not because it is being retrained, but because it is being refined.

Detection Architecture

Input Channel 01

EO / IR Optical Feed

Continuous calibrated visible-spectrum or thermal infrared image stream. Used as a precision measurement instrument — not a classifier input. Works day, night, and in adverse conditions.

Input Channel 02

Kinematic Track Cue

Initial angular bearing from upstream radar, RF sensor, or operator designation. OTIS maps this into a calibrated 3D ray using its own sensor geometry — the cue locates; OTIS identifies.

Input Channel 03

Geometric Observables

Angular subtense, optical flow consistency, thermal profile geometry, specular flash rate, and range-estimated physical dimensions — extracted from the optical stream against the cued track.

Input Channel 04

Consistency Scoring

All observables are scored against physics-constrained threat models. The weighted composite output routes to: threat confirmed, suppress/monitor, or flag for human review.

Categorical Discriminators

Three observables are geometrically categorical — derived from physical laws, not statistical learning. They generalize to any airframe, including novel designs never seen before.

Kinematic Stabilization

Any drone with a flight controller exhibits mechanically stabilized center-of-mass translation. Biological flight is kinematically noisy at frame-by-frame resolution. A 1–3 second optical flow measurement separates these populations categorically.

Rotational Mechanical Signature

Multi-rotor UAS generate a periodic optical flicker from propeller blades at their rotation frequency — directly measurable in calibrated EO imagery. No avian species produces an equivalent signal. Categorical. Not probabilistic.

Thermal Asymmetry Geometry

Drone motors produce concentrated high-delta-T hotspots. Avian thermal signatures are anatomically symmetric and biologically smooth. A spatial entropy metric over the thermal blob cleanly separates these classes at operationally relevant ranges.
