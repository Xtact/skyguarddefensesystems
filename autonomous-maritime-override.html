Bypassing Maritime Optical Scattering: USV Bridge Detection via Spatial Skeletonization

By SkyGuard_Lead | SkyGuard Defense Systems (InSitu Labs) | Denver, CO

Unmanned Surface Vessels (USVs) and autonomous maritime navigation systems operate in the most hostile optical environments on earth. Heavy mist, dusk fog, and water-surface light reflection create massive optical scattering. Standard RGB feeds become useless, and traditional LiDAR suffers from severe particulate return-bounce, registering mist as a solid wall.

When a USV approaches a low-clearance river bridge at dusk, the optical matrix cannot differentiate the concrete structure from the surrounding fog. The vessel maintains speed on a collision course.

O.T.I.S. penetrates the mist. We decouple the particulate scattering from the actual physical mass, rendering a live spatial mesh of the bridge structure before the cameras can even see it.
